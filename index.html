Description

This is a seminar; it is also a workshop, laboratory, and playground where the cohort engage in collaborative artistic exploration with each other. This seminar will explore the intersection of performance art, sound, and technology. Through readings, discussions, and creative exercises, we will investigate the artistic potential of digital technologies, as well as the ethical implications and socio-political potentiality through the lens of Posthumanism and Whitehead’s process ontology.
We will begin by considering the concept of the "virtual" as it relates to performance art, drawing on the philosophical work of Gilles Deleuze and Henri Bergson. In common parlance, “virtual” is often synonymous with “digital,” which implies a cartesian disembodiment.mind-body dualism; see René Descartes, The Discourse on Method (1637) However, we will critically examine this opposition between virtuality and embodiment and seek ways to bridge the two in the process of artistic inquiry.
Next, we will explore the role of sound in performance art, looking at how sound can be used to create atmosphere, embodiment, and transformation, both within and as a performance. We will examine the use of generative music made with modular synthesizers, among other sound design techniques, and consider how these elements can work in tandem with performance art in a multidisciplinary conversation. Potential workshops may take place in the Studio for Electronic Art (VCAM 202).
We will also examine the use of new technologies in performance art, including virtual environments (AR/VR), machine learning, and artificial intelligence.Depending on the cohort’s level of expertise in computer science, this topic may be modified to replace program implementations with tutorials on some intuitive programming interfaces such as PureData and Max/MSP. We will explore how these technologies can be used to create new possibilities for embodied experiences, while also acknowledging their limitations and ethical implications.
A central focus throughout the seminar is the relationship between virtuality, embodiment, and identity, particularly how these themes intersect with social and political issues. We will examine the works of contemporary artists who explore these themes through their performances, such as Stelarc, LaTurbo Avedon, and others.
The seminar will culminate in a collaborative multi-media performance art installation to be presented to the campus community and/or beyond.
In this seminar, performance art is not only the topic of research but also the methodology—each session will be primarily centered on embodied explorations. No prior experience with performance art is necessary, and the seminar activities will be modified should there be a need to accommodate different abilities. Given the interdisciplinary nature of the topic, this seminar will appeal to students from a wide variety of disciplines, including History of Art, Visual Studies, Fine Arts, Music, Computer Science, Anthropology, Sociology, Philosophy, etc.

Syllabus

Note: all readings listed are suggestions; please feel free to peruse chapter(s) of interest.

Introduction

What is virtuality? What is being vs. becoming? How to become a virtual body?

Readings:

Gilles Deleuze, "The Actual and the Virtual"
Henri Bergson, Matter and Memory
Erin Manning, Always More Than One: Individuation's Dance

Posthuman Performativity

How can we approach “embodiment” from a Posthuman framework?

Readings:

Judith Butler, "Performative Acts and Gender Constitution: An Essay in Phenomenology and Feminist Theory"
N. Katherine Hayles, How We Became Posthuman: Virtual Bodies in Cybernetics, Literature, and Informatics

Sound & Performance Art

How can sound be used to create atmosphere and embodiment within performance art? How does sound perform as a body of its own?

Readings:

Brandon LaBelle, Acoustic Territorities: Sound Culture and Every Day Life
Frances Dyson, Sounding New Media: Immersion and Embodiment in the Arts and Culture

Modular Synthesis & Generative Music
